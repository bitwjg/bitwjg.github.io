Controlled reasoning-efficiency study

Do Larger Reasoning Models Actually Use Tokens More Efficiently?

From an Interview Claim to 97,024 Experimental Rows: An AI-Collaborative Auto Research Practice

July 17, 2026 · Research Notes

A 97,024-row comparison across GPT-OSS and Qwen3.5 finds strong but family-conditional evidence—not a universal scaling law.

The claim that started the project

Can a stronger reasoning model reach the same or better answer with fewer visible reasoning tokens?

Three OpenAI researchers approached that question from complementary directions. Paraphrasing their interview remarks:

Noam BrownMore thinking is not automatically useful; only a sufficiently capable model can turn extra test-time compute into better answers.
Yann DuboisEfficient reasoning resembles expert search: select the promising path early instead of spending tokens on many dead ends.
Josh McGrathThe useful movement on a quality–token plane is up and left: higher quality with fewer tokens, not merely a higher score.

Taken together, these remarks imply—but do not prove—our hypothesis: as a model becomes stronger, it may internalize better path selection and move the quality–visible-reasoning frontier up and to the left, reaching equal or better quality with fewer explicit reasoning tokens.

That is plausible, but not automatic. Parameter count alone does not guarantee a shorter trace or lower total inference FLOPs. A larger model can score higher simply because it thinks longer; it can also spend thousands of tokens reasoning and never emit a valid final answer.

Within the same model family, under matched tasks, output budgets, decoding rules, and repeated seeds, does a larger model move the quality–reasoning-token frontier outward?

The design follows directly from that question: compare two sizes inside each family, fix the task and generation protocol, sweep multiple output budgets, repeat each problem, and admit token-efficiency evidence only after final-validity, token-partition, and truncation gates pass.

GPT-5.5 and GPT-5.6 were research collaborators throughout the project. They helped trace and challenge the hypothesis, design the matrix, audit remote runs, diagnose measurement failures, analyze accepted artifacts, and prepare the report, paper draft, and this article. They are not evidence for the conclusion: every empirical claim below comes from logged jobs and validated result files.

From a broad model screen to two controlled families

The project did not begin with two hand-picked winners. The initial candidate pool included GPT-OSS, Qwen3.5 Dense, Ministral-3, Qwen3, and GLM-4.5. We piloted each family, then asked whether it could support an interpretable within-family scale comparison.

Admission contract. A family needed two meaningfully comparable sizes; matched tokenizer, template, parser, and generation protocol; enough task, budget, and repeated-sampling coverage; and final-validity, token-partition, and truncation quality sufficient for trustworthy token measurement.

GPT-OSS and Qwen3.5 Dense survived that screen. Ministral-3, Qwen3, and GLM-4.5 were not removed because their results were inconvenient: they exposed measurement failure, architecture confounding, genuine scale reversal, and inference-budget interaction. The evidence scope was frozen by comparability and measurement validity, not by which family confirmed the hypothesis.

GPT-OSS: the clearest positive result

In all five eligible fixed-budget comparisons, GPT-OSS 120B uses fewer thinking tokens than 20B. In four, it is also more accurate. All four quality-matched frontier points show token savings.

  • MATH500: 95.8% → 97.2%, with 50.6% fewer thinking tokens.
  • GPQA-Diamond: 66.9% → 73.1%, with 60.9% fewer thinking tokens.
  • AIME 2025: 74.2% → 83.3%, with 48.5% fewer thinking tokens.

AIME 2024 is the useful counterexample: thinking falls by 50.2%, but accuracy drops from 84.6% to 81.7%. Scale does not dominate every task.

Qwen3.5: positive, but narrower

On MATH500 at 32K, Qwen3.5 27B improves from 93.3% to 98.0% while using 18.7% fewer thinking tokens and 18.7% fewer total tokens than 9B.

AIME accuracy also favors the larger model—70.8% to 79.2% on AIME 2024 and 62.1% to 77.9% on AIME 2025—but those cells fail final-validity or truncation gates. They remain strict-accuracy evidence and are excluded from the uncensored token-efficiency claim.

The parser question

The Qwen3.5 AIME failures initially looked like a parser bug. Artifact inspection showed that they were mainly model behavior. Qwen3.5 preserves a valid thinking/answer partition but often consumes the budget before emitting a terminal answer. The dominant failure is therefore an incomplete reasoning-to-final transition, not a global extraction bug.

That diagnosis matters because blindly rerunning the same configuration would not automatically fix a systematic finalization pattern. We retained strict accuracy, censored token-efficiency claims where required, and avoided selecting only well-finalized traces.

Ministral-3: not merely a parser bug

The K=8 runs completed, but no final comparison passed the full validity, partition, and truncation contract. The official parser correctly handled completed streams. The dominant AIME failure occurred earlier: the model spent its budget in reasoning without emitting a terminal answer. The parser exposed these incomplete streams; it did not create them.

MATH500 at 32K also contains a substantive reversal: 14B reaches only 7.0% accuracy versus 31.6% for 8B. Parser repair alone therefore cannot recover the hypothesis. The supported diagnosis combines reasoning-to-final measurement failure with a real smaller-model advantage; template, post-training, and inference-setting mechanisms remain unresolved.

The technical report makes this result less mysterious without proving its cause. Ministral 3 is built by sequentially pruning, continuing training, and distilling 14B→8B→3B from Mistral Small 3.1, so 8B and 14B are not independent, recipe-matched scale points. Its ablations also show that teacher choice materially affects math. Distillation path and teacher–student compatibility are therefore plausible sources of non-monotonic scaling, not established explanations for our reversal.

The finalization link is more direct: the Reasoning recipe combines short/long-CoT SFT, GRPO, and ODPO, and the authors increased RL generation length from 32K to 80K after observing non-trivial truncation. Our 32K gate is probing a long-reasoning sensitivity documented by the recipe, not an arbitrary parser defect.

Qwen3: successful execution, but scale is not a clean variable

The pilot ran and parsing was not the limiting factor. Qwen3-235B-A22B improved average AIME accuracy and used fewer total tokens than 30B-A3B, although pass@8 was slightly lower. On MATH500, 30B-A3B was both more accurate (94.2% versus 89.8%) and shorter (3.34K versus 4.04K total tokens).

This comparison mixes total parameters, active MoE parameters, routing/architecture, post-training, and incomplete matrix scope. It supports a benchmark-dependent reversal, not a clean parameter-scale claim. Community comparisons of MoE families should report total and active parameters, routing, and post-training separately.

The Qwen3 report confirms the confounding. 30B-A3B has 3B active parameters and 48 layers; 235B-A22B has 22B active parameters and 94 layers. The lightweight 30B model also receives off-policy and on-policy strong-to-weak distillation, while the flagship 235B follows the full four-stage reasoning/general-RL pipeline. A distilled smaller policy may compress high-value teacher trajectories more effectively, but our pilot cannot prove that mechanism.

Official inference differs too. Qwen3's thinking-budget control inserts an instruction to stop thinking and answer rather than hard-stopping at the threshold, and official AIME evaluation uses 38,912 tokens. Our hard 32K cap had no forced reasoning-to-final conversion. This may explain some AIME missing finals, but it cannot explain the MATH500 reversal.

GLM-4.5: a counterexample plus inference-setting sensitivity

On 32K AIME, GLM-4.5-Air beats full GLM-4.5 on avg@8 (64.4% versus 55.0%) and pass@8 (91.7% versus 83.3%), while the larger model truncates more often. Valid token partitions rule out a global parser failure.

GPQA reverses direction, while both models remain below the final-validity gate. The AIME smaller-model win is real under the tested setup, but its cross-task interpretation is entangled with cap and exit-reasoning behavior. The informative follow-up is a small ablation over budgets, templates, stop conditions, and forced finalization—not a blind rerun.

The GLM-4.5 report describes Air and full GLM as 106B/12B-active and 355B/32B-active MoE systems. Post-training first builds reasoning, agent, and chat experts, then self-distills them into a hybrid thinking/direct-response policy. The authors found gradual output-length expansion difficult to recover from and therefore ran final reasoning RL directly at 64K. Our 32K cap can plausibly amplify truncation and exit differences.

But cap mismatch is not sufficient. In our existing seed-0-only 64K diagnostic, mean strict AIME24/25 accuracy still favors Air (55.0% versus 50.0%), with truncation around 23.3%–30.0%. This slice is not a replacement for K=8, but doubling the cap did not restore larger-model dominance. Hybrid distillation, active-scale differences, or long-reasoning policy may still contribute.

A reusable diagnostic order

  1. Separate completed execution from eligible measurement.
  2. Unit-test parsers on complete streams and control tasks.
  3. Keep strict accuracy while censoring ineligible token lengths.
  4. Sweep output caps before attributing failure to capability.
  5. Treat active MoE scale and post-training as confounders.
  6. Preserve valid smaller-model wins as counterexamples.

“Excluded from the main result” can mean invalid measurement, a non-identifiable comparison, or a genuinely better smaller model. Those are scientifically different outcomes.

What I believe after the experiment

Within some controlled model families, a larger model can achieve equal or better quality with fewer visible reasoning tokens. The effect is strong for GPT-OSS and present but narrower for Qwen3.5.

A 50.6% reduction in visible reasoning for GPT-OSS MATH500 and an 18.7% reduction for Qwen3.5 MATH500 are large enough to matter operationally. But they are not a universal law of parameter scale.

The more general lesson is methodological: reasoning models should be evaluated on a quality–token frontier with explicit final-validity, token-partition, and truncation gates. Accuracy alone hides whether a model merely thinks longer; token length alone can reward responses that never finish.

Limitations

  • The comparison is observational, not causal; size is entangled with architecture, data, and post-training.
  • Visible reasoning tokens are not total compute. A larger model can still cost more per token.
  • Tokenizers differ, so raw token counts are compared only within a family.
  • The frontier is sampled at four discrete budgets.
  • Censoring improves validity but leaves fewer admissible Qwen3.5 comparisons.
  • GPT-OSS high-effort remains an incomplete auxiliary slice.

这个项目从一个直觉开始

更强的推理模型，能否用更少的可见推理 token，得到相同或更好的答案？

OpenAI 三位研究员分别从不同角度触及了这个问题。把他们在访谈中的表述转述如下：

Noam Brown“想得更久”本身并不保证更好；只有模型具备足够能力，额外测试时计算才能转化为答案质量。
Yann Dubois高效推理像专家搜索：尽早选择最有希望的方向，而不把 token 消耗在许多死路上。
Josh McGrath模型进步应该在“质量—token”平面上向左上移动：质量更高、token 更少，而不只是分数更高。

三条观点合在一起，导出但并不直接证明我们的假设：随着模型变强，它可能内化更好的路径选择能力，把质量—可见推理前沿推向左上方——用更少的显式推理 token，达到相同或更高的质量。

这个假设很合理，但并不自动成立。参数量更大不等于推理轨迹必然更短，也不等于总推理 FLOPs 更低。大模型可能只是因为“想得更久”才拿到更高分，也可能生成几千个推理 token 后仍没有给出有效的最终答案。

在同一模型家族内，当任务、输出预算、解码规则和重复种子都保持一致时，增大模型规模是否会把“质量—推理 token”前沿推向更优的位置？

实验设计直接从这个问题推出：在每个家族内比较两个规模，固定任务和生成协议，扫描多档输出预算，对每道题进行重复采样；只有最终答案有效率、token 分区和截断率都通过预设门禁时，才允许进入 token 效率比较。

GPT-5.5 和 GPT-5.6 作为研究协作者参与了整个流程：追溯和质疑原始假设、设计实验矩阵、审计远端任务、诊断测量失败、分析验收后的产物，以及协助准备实验报告、论文初稿和这篇文章。它们本身不是结论的证据；下面每个实证数字都来自可复核的任务日志和通过验收的结果文件。

从广泛选型到两个正式实验家族

项目并不是从两个“答案正确”的家族开始。最初的候选池包括 GPT-OSS、Qwen3.5 Dense、Ministral-3、Qwen3 和 GLM-4.5。我们先做 pilot，再判断每个家族能否形成可解释的同家族尺度对照。

进入正式矩阵的条件。同家族内至少有两个可比较规模；tokenizer、模板、解析器和生成协议能够匹配；任务、预算和重复采样覆盖足够；final validity、token 分区与截断率允许可靠测量 token。

筛选后，GPT-OSS 和 Qwen3.5 Dense 形成最清楚的受控比较。Ministral-3、Qwen3 和 GLM-4.5 并不是因为结果“不漂亮”才被删除：它们分别暴露了测量失效、架构混杂、真实尺度反转与推理预算交互。证据范围是按可比性与测量有效性冻结的，而不是按哪个家族支持假设来挑选。

GPT-OSS：最清晰的正向结果

在 5 个通过门禁的固定预算比较中，GPT-OSS 120B 的 thinking tokens 全部少于 20B；其中 4 个单元同时具有更高准确率。4 个等质量前沿点也全部显示 token 节省。

  • MATH500：95.8% → 97.2%，thinking tokens 减少 50.6%；
  • GPQA-Diamond：66.9% → 73.1%，thinking tokens 减少 60.9%；
  • AIME 2025：74.2% → 83.3%，thinking tokens 减少 48.5%。

AIME 2024 是一个有价值的反例：thinking tokens 虽然减少了 50.2%，准确率却从 84.6% 降到 81.7%。规模并不会在每个任务上都形成支配。

Qwen3.5：方向一致，但证据更窄

在 MATH500 的 32K 设置下，Qwen3.5 27B 的准确率从 93.3% 提升到 98.0%，同时比 9B 少用 18.7% thinking tokens 和 18.7% total tokens。

AIME 的准确率同样更偏向大模型：AIME 2024 从 70.8% 提升到 79.2%，AIME 2025 从 62.1% 提升到 77.9%。但这些单元没有通过最终答案有效率或截断率门禁，因此仍是有效的严格准确率结果，却不进入未删失的 token 效率结论。

到底是不是解析器的问题？

Qwen3.5 的 AIME 失败一开始看上去很像解析器问题。逐条检查产物后发现，主要原因其实是模型行为。thinking/answer 分区本身有效，但长推理经常耗尽预算，来不及输出终止性的最终答案。主要失败因此是 reasoning-to-final 转换没有完成，而不是全局性的抽取错误。

这个诊断很重要，因为用同一配置大规模补跑，并不会自动解决系统性的收尾行为。因此我们保留严格准确率，在需要时删失 token 效率结论，也不通过只保留“收尾良好”的样本来人为修复数据。

Ministral-3：不是单纯的解析器故障

K=8 运行覆盖完整，但没有比较通过有效性、分区与截断率的联合门禁。官方解析器能正确处理完整生成流；AIME 的主要失败发生得更早——模型把预算消耗在 reasoning 中，却没有输出终止性的 final。解析器只是暴露了不完整流，并不是根因。

MATH500 32K 还出现实质性反转：14B 只有 7.0%，8B 为 31.6%。因此不能用“修好 parser，大模型就会赢”解释。现有证据同时指向 reasoning-to-final 测量失效和真实的小模型优势；模板、后训练或推理配置的具体责任仍需消融。

技术报告让这个反转更容易理解，但还不能完成因果归因。Ministral 3 从 Mistral Small 3.1 出发，按 14B→8B→3B 依次剪枝、继续训练和蒸馏；8B 与 14B 并不是从头独立训练的等配方尺度点。报告消融还显示 teacher 选择会显著影响数学能力。因此，级联蒸馏路径和 teacher—student 匹配是非单调尺度结果的合理机制线索，但不是已证实原因。

对未收尾问题，报告证据更直接：Reasoning 配方结合 short/long-CoT SFT、GRPO 和 ODPO；作者观察到不可忽略的截断后，把 RL 最大生成长度从 32K 提高到 80K。我们的 32K 门禁确实撞上了该配方已知的长推理敏感性，而不是偶然 parser bug。

Qwen3：运行成功，但“规模”不是干净变量

pilot 执行成功，解析也不是限制因素。235B-A22B 在 AIME 上平均准确率更高、total tokens 更少，但 pass@8 略低；MATH500 上，30B-A3B 同时更准（94.2% 对 89.8%）且更短（3.34K 对 4.04K total tokens）。

这组比较混合了总参数、MoE 激活参数、路由/架构、后训练和不完整矩阵范围。它支持“结果依赖 benchmark”，却不能干净归因于参数规模。社区比较 MoE 家族时，应分别报告总参数、激活参数、路由和后训练协议。

Qwen3 技术报告进一步确认了混杂：30B-A3B 是 3B 激活、48 层，235B-A22B 是 22B 激活、94 层。更关键的是，轻量 30B 接受 off-policy 与 on-policy 强到弱蒸馏，而旗舰 235B 走完整四阶段 reasoning/general-RL 流程。小模型可能把 teacher 的高价值轨迹压缩成更稳定的策略，但这仍是解释假设。

官方推理协议也不同。thinking budget 达到阈值时会插入停止思考并作答的指令，而不是直接硬截断；官方 AIME 评测使用 38,912 token。我们的硬 32K cap 没有强制 reasoning-to-final，这可能解释一部分 AIME 缺失 final，却解释不了 MATH500 的完整反转。

GLM-4.5：真实反例叠加推理配置敏感性

32K AIME 上，GLM-4.5-Air 的 avg@8（64.4% 对 55.0%）和 pass@8（91.7% 对 83.3%）都高于完整模型，且大模型截断更多。两者 token 分区有效，因此不能归咎于全局 parser 故障。

GPQA 的尺度方向相反，同时两个模型 final validity 都低于门禁。AIME 上小模型胜出是真实观察，但跨任务解释受到预算与退出推理行为影响。正确的后续实验是对预算、模板、终止条件和强制收尾做小规模消融，而不是盲目补跑同配置。

GLM-4.5 报告显示 Air 与完整模型分别是 106B/12B 激活和 355B/32B 激活的 MoE。后训练先构建 reasoning、agent 和 chat 专家，再把它们自蒸馏成 thinking/direct-response 混合策略。作者还发现逐步扩展输出长度会造成难以恢复的退化，因此最终直接在 64K 输出长度上做 reasoning RL。我们的 32K cap 很可能放大了截断和退出差异。

但预算错配不是充分解释。我们已有的仅 seed0 64K 诊断中，AIME24/25 平均严格准确率仍由 Air 领先（55.0% 对 50.0%），截断仍约为 23.3%–30.0%。这不能替代 K=8，却说明把 cap 翻倍并没有恢复大模型优势；hybrid 蒸馏、激活规模或长推理策略仍可能共同作用。

一套可复用的诊断顺序

  1. 区分“任务跑完”和“测量合格”；
  2. 用完整生成流和控制任务单测 parser；
  3. 保留严格准确率，同时删失不合格 token 长度；
  4. 扫描多档预算，再判断是否属于能力问题；
  5. 把 MoE 激活规模与后训练视为混杂变量；
  6. 保留有效的小模型胜出，把它当作真实反例。

所以，“没有进入主结果”可能意味着测量无效、因果比较不可识别，或者小模型确实更好。这三种科学结论不能混为一谈。

实验之后，我如何理解这个问题

在某些受控模型家族内，更大的模型可以用更少的可见推理 token 达到相同或更高质量。这个效应在 GPT-OSS 上很强，在 Qwen3.5 上方向一致但证据更窄。

GPT-OSS 在 MATH500 上减少 50.6% 的可见推理，Qwen3.5 减少 18.7%，都具有实际意义。但它们仍然不是“参数规模越大，token 效率越高”的普适定律。

更一般的方法论结论是：推理模型应该在一条质量—token 前沿上被评估，并明确设置最终答案有效率、token 分区有效率和截断率门禁。只看准确率，会掩盖模型是否只是想得更久；只看 token 长度，又可能错误奖励那些根本没有完成回答的响应。

局限

  • 这是观察性比较，不是因果实验；规模与架构、数据和后训练方法相互纠缠。
  • 可见 reasoning tokens 不是总计算量；大模型的单 token 成本仍可能更高。
  • 不同家族 tokenizer 不同，因此原始 token 数只在家族内部比较。
  • 等质量前沿只采样了四档离散预算。
  • 删失提高了可信度，但减少了 Qwen3.5 的可用比较。
  • GPT-OSS high-effort 仍是一个不完整的辅助切片。
